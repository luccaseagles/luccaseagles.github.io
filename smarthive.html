Smart Hive

Problem

Bee population in the UK has dropped by one third in the past decade. Insects, such as bees, are the most effective crop pollinators, as 84% of crop species depend on pollinating insects. A further significant drop in bee population would cost billions in food sales across the EU. The causes are multifaceted including pesticides, mite infestation, availability of food, and climate change. However, beekeepers only have access to a limited amount of information on their hiveâ€™s health, as inspecting a hive too frequently disturbs the bees. Moreover, it is often challenging for an apiarist to find a financially viable treatment in order to improve hive health. Our objective is to design an electronic system which can assist bee keepers in identifying and treating problems within the hive.
